Frequently Asked Questions

What is NEPSE?

NEPSE stands for Nepal Stock Exchange. It is the only stock exchange in Nepal, established in 1993, and serves as a platform for buying and selling shares of publicly listed companies.

How can I invest in NEPSE?

To invest in NEPSE, you need to open a trading account with a registered broker. After that, you can buy and sell shares through the broker's trading platform or through direct interaction.

What types of securities are traded on NEPSE?

NEPSE trades a variety of securities, including common shares, preferred shares, debentures, and mutual funds. Investors can choose from a range of options based on their investment strategy.

How are stock prices determined in NEPSE?

Stock prices in NEPSE are determined by supply and demand in the market. Factors such as company performance, market sentiment, and economic conditions can influence stock prices.

What are the trading hours for NEPSE?

The trading hours for NEPSE are typically from 11:00 AM to 3:00 PM, Sunday to Thursday. However, it's important to check for any holiday schedules or changes in trading hours.

What is the NEPSE index?

The NEPSE index is a market index that measures the performance of all the listed companies in the exchange. It serves as a barometer of the market's overall performance and investor sentiment.

What are the listing requirements for companies in NEPSE?

To be listed on NEPSE, companies must meet specific criteria set by the exchange, including minimum paid-up capital, financial performance standards, and corporate governance requirements.

How can I access real-time data for NEPSE?

You can access real-time data for NEPSE through various online platforms, financial news websites, or by using dedicated applications that provide live market updates.

What are the risks associated with investing in NEPSE?

Investing in NEPSE carries risks, including market volatility, economic changes, and specific company performance issues. It's important to conduct thorough research and consider diversifying your portfolio to mitigate risks.
